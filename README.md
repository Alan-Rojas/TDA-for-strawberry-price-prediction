## Although strawberry is considered a stationary product, its price prediction is difficutlt for businesses as many factors influence its behavior. 
This project tackles down this problem for berries (strawberries, blueberries, blackberries and raspberries) comparing two different approaches to find the most accurate for price forecasting.

The first approach is sort of a classical scope with time-series-based models (like ARIMA) with the goal of picking up patterns and understanding the behavior so forecast can be executedly accuraely. 
On the other hand, a modern-mathematical approach is propposed leveraging TDA (Topological Data Analysis) with probabilistic techniques such as Markov Chains and Machine Learning. 

Ultimately, the goal of the project shifted from accurately predicting berries price, to comparing two different forecasting methods on stationary products with data noise.

Which approach worked better? Do modern-mathematical approaches out-run classical models? That, dear reader, is the question.\
**Feel free to dive into the scripts and files to answer that question yourself!**
